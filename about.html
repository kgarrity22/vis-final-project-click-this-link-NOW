Why did we choose this?

Created by Kathleen, Dan, Kim, and Kieran


The problem we are attempting to demonstrate and offer some solutions to is evaluating restaurants in Boston based on attributes such as review ratings, cuisine, price point, location, etc. We were interested in exploring whether the trend of reviews correlates to COVID trends in Boston.

Here is a bit more about our work

This group has seen the fluctuation and struggle within the restaurant industry within these past 8 months. Restaurants faced severe pressure during the beginning of the COVID crisis as they were forced to shut down without a glimmer of hope as to when they would be able to reopen. Then, they were allowed to reopen only for takeout, thus for many businesses this created another issue of shifting their business model to accommodate the new rules - partnering with more delivery apps that charge insane fees, having to create new and safe ways for customers to pick up their orders, and attempting to keep all their employees employed or at least offer some sort of compensation. Understandinng how the COVID crisis affected restaurants’ ratings and whether or not customers were more forgiving and understanding of shortcomings that restaurants may have had during these times. And with the new 9:30PM curfew that restaurants and bars in Boston must follow, the restaurant industry will once again need to be innovative and quick-thinking.

How did we collect our Data?

Click here to see data

We used a dataset published by Harvard Dataverse. According to this published study, this dataset was created by generating a list of Boston restaurants by searching up their zip codes on Yelp, and verifying that the restaurant’s address falls within Boston’s boundaries. This data was processed and scraped by BARI. The study includes multiple datasets (YELP.Reviews, YELP.Restaurants, and YELP.CT) which all contain different information about the restaurants. We were able to find this study and download the separate datasets to form our own

The dataset we found was scraped directly from these Yelp reviews, which ultimately fit exactly what we were intending to represent. The dataset’s attributes gives us flexibility to evaluate the quality of the restaurants, type of food served, price point, and overall reviews of the restaurants. The dataset also gives us the address for each restaurant, which will give us more options to use this location data for a map visualization.

Additionally, we need to convert the addresses of the restaurants given in the dataset to coordinates (latitude and longitude) in order for them to function with the MapBox we have integregated. This was done using a mass Excel formula which was able to quickly all addreses.
